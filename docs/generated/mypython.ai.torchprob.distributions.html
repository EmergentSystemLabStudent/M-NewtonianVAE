class mypython.ai.torchprob.distributions.gmm.GMM(pis: Union[None, int, float, Tensor] = None, mus: Union[None, int, float, Tensor] = None, sigmas: Union[None, int, float, Tensor] = None)

Bases: Distribution

Gaussian Mixture Model

\[\begin{array}{ll} p(\boldsymbol{x} \mid \boldsymbol{\Theta}) = \displaystyle \sum_{k=1}^K \pi_k \, \mathcal{N}(\boldsymbol{x} \mid \boldsymbol{\mu}_k, \boldsymbol{\Sigma}_k) \end{array}\]

“Normal (Gaussian) distribution” is not multivariate normal distribution.

Constraint:

\[\begin{array}{ll} \displaystyle \sum_{k=1}^K \pi_k = 1 \end{array}\]
Inputs: pi, mus, sigmas

  • pi: tensor of shape \((*, K)\)

  • mus: tensor of shape \((*, K, D)\)

  • sigmas: tensor of shape \((*, K, D)\)

where:

\[\begin{split}\begin{aligned} K ={} & \text{number of mixtures} \\ D ={} & \text{number of features} \\ \end{aligned}\end{split}\]

mypython.ai.torchprob.distributions.normal.log_normal(x: Tensor, mu: Tensor, sigma: Tensor) → Tensor

Log-likelihood of a Gaussian distribution

\[\begin{split}\begin{array}{ll} \\ \log \mathcal{N}(x \mid \mu, \sigma^2) = - \frac{1}{2} \left( \exp \left( \frac{(x - \mu)^2}{\sigma^2} \right) + 2 \ln \sigma + \ln 2\pi \right) \end{array}\end{split}\]

If the sigma is fixed at 1, This equation is equivalent to \(- \frac{1}{2} (x - \mu)^2 + C\). This is equivalent to sum of squared errors (SSE) and is widely used as a mathematical expression for reconstruction error. That is, this \(\mu\) is the image (, etc.) that is reconstructed.
